Part 1: Indemnity and Guarantee

Contract of Indemnity (Section 124)

Definition: A contract by which one party promises to save the other from loss caused to him by the conduct of the promisor himself, or by the conduct of any other person.

Parties:

Indemnifier: The person who gives the promise to make good the loss.
Indemnity Holder: The person who is protected against loss.
A promises to indemnify B for any legal proceedings filed by C against B regarding a certain property.
Case Law: **Gajanan Moreshwar Parelkar v. Moreshwar Madan Mantri** (1942) - This case established that the indemnity holder can compel the indemnifier to cover the loss even before the actual loss is incurred, emphasizing the proactive nature of indemnity.
2

Contract of Guarantee (Section 126)

Definition: A contract to perform the promise, or discharge the liability, of a third person in case of his default.

Parties:

Surety: The person who gives the guarantee.
Principal Debtor: The person in respect of whose default the guarantee is given.
Creditor: The person to whom the guarantee is given.
A requests C to lend money to B. A promises C that if B fails to repay, A will.
Case Law: **State Bank of India v. V. Rama Rao** (2000) - This case illustrates that the liability of the surety is co-extensive with that of the principal debtor, but the creditor can proceed against the surety directly without first suing the principal debtor.
3

Distinction Between Indemnity and Guarantee

Feature Contract of Indemnity Contract of Guarantee
Parties Two parties: Indemnifier & Indemnity Holder Three parties: Surety, Principal Debtor & Creditor
Liability The liability is primary and independent. The liability is secondary, arising only on the default of the Principal Debtor.
Promise There is only one contract. There are three contracts: between the Creditor and Principal Debtor; between the Creditor and Surety; and an implied contract between the Surety and Principal Debtor.
Purpose To save a person from loss. To secure the creditor's debt or performance of a promise.
4

Rights of an Indemnity Holder

Right to Recover Damages: The indemnity holder is entitled to recover all damages he may be compelled to pay in any suit in respect of any matter to which the promise to indemnify applies (Section 125).
Right to Recover Costs: The indemnity holder has the right to recover all costs that he may have been compelled to pay in bringing or defending a suit, provided he acted as a prudent person and did not contravene the promisor's orders.
Right to Recover Sums Paid on Compromise: The indemnity holder is entitled to recover all sums which he may have paid under the terms of any compromise of any such suit, if the compromise was not contrary to the promisor's orders.

Part 2: Rights and Duties of Partners

Introduction to Partnership

Definition: A partnership is the relation between persons who have agreed to share the profits of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all (Indian Partnership Act, 1932, Section 4).

Key Characteristics:

Two or more persons.
Agreement to share profits.
Mutual agency.
Business must be carried on.
Case Law: **K.D. Sharma v. Commissioner of Income Tax** (1998) - This case re-affirmed that the existence of a business and a profit-sharing agreement are essential elements for a valid partnership.
6

Rights of Partners

Right to Participate in the Business: Every partner has the right to take part in the conduct of the business (Section 12(a)).
Right to be Consulted: Every partner has a right to express his opinion before any matter is decided by the firm (Section 12(c)).
Right to Share Profits: Partners are entitled to share the profits and contribute equally to the losses of the firm (Section 13(b)).
Right to Remuneration: A partner is not entitled to receive remuneration for taking part in the conduct of the business (Section 13(a)).
Right to Access Books of Accounts: Every partner has a right to have access to and inspect and copy any of the books of the firm (Section 12(d)).
7

Duties of Partners

Duty to Carry on Business Honestly: Partners are bound to carry on the business of the firm to the greatest common advantage, to be just and faithful to each other, and to render true accounts (Section 9).
Duty to Indemnify for Fraud: Every partner must indemnify the firm for any loss caused to it by his willful neglect or fraud.
Duty to Render True Accounts: Partners must provide full information and true accounts of all things affecting the firm to any other partner or his legal representative (Section 9).
Duty to Not Compete: A partner who carries on a competing business must account for and pay over to the firm all profits made by him in that business.
8

Conclusion

Contracts of indemnity and guarantee serve distinct purposes, one to protect against loss, the other to secure a debt.
The rights and duties of partners are legally defined to ensure a fair and equitable relationship within the firm.
Understanding these concepts is crucial for anyone involved in commercial or business agreements.
